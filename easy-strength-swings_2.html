40 Sessions at a Glance
Never Miss a Workout
Show up five days a week for 40 sessions. Even if you're tired — do the minimum. The swing alone counts on a bad day. The habit of showing up is the actual training stimulus.
Never Miss a Lift
No failed reps. Every set ends before failure. You should never wonder if you'll complete the rep. Leave 2–3 reps in the tank always. This is how you train hard five days a week without burning out.
Two Reps Below Max — Always
Pick a weight you could lift for 7, then only do 5. This isn't weakness — it's the mechanism. Consistent submaximal work accumulates into serious strength over 40 sessions.
The Five Movements
Push · Pull · Hinge · Squat · Carry. Cover all five across the week. The swing covers the hinge and conditions the posterior chain — it earns a daily place without crowding other patterns.
The Swing is Non-Negotiable
Every session includes swings. Dan John calls it the perfect exercise. It teaches power, reinforces the hip hinge, and conditions without grinding joints. There is no reason to skip it.
Progression Rules
Trap bar & OHP: +2.5–5kg when easy for 2 sessions.
Pull-ups: add belt weight when BW 5s feel controlled.
Goblet squat: keep it heavy — make heavy feel normal.
Ab roller: 1 more rep or longer extension, not speed.
Carries: farmer → suitcase → waiter / rack.
Swings: bell weight → rep schemes → density.
